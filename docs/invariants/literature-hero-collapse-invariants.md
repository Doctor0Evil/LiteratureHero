## Invariant list for Literature%Hero

1. **Unity / Hive Mind**  
   - **Fictional Trait:** Individuals dissolve into a unified collective consciousness or absolute ideological “hive,” with dissent framed as treason or insanity.  
   - **Historical Inspiration:** Totalitarian collectivism that demands fusion of self and state/party/leader.  
   - **Real‑World Pattern:** Mass youth or party movements that enforce ideological purity through mutual surveillance, struggle sessions, and ritualized loyalty.

2. **Tech Gatekeeping / Esoteric Control**  
   - **Fictional Trait:** A small technocratic priesthood controls critical infrastructure (reactors, AIs, orbital weapons) and decides who may access or even understand it.  
   - **Historical Inspiration:** Secret police, nuclear and cryptographic elites, closed military research complexes.  
   - **Real‑World Pattern:** Regimes where technical knowledge, encryption, or weapons programs are guarded as “state mysteries,” creating a power bottleneck around engineers and operators.

3. **Utopian Principles Weaponized**  
   - **Fictional Trait:** Movements begin with lofty egalitarian, ecological, or spiritual goals that justify increasingly absolutist control “for the greater good.”  
   - **Historical Inspiration:** Revolutionary regimes and communal experiments that slide from reform to repression.  
   - **Real‑World Pattern:** Groups that start as social‑justice, anti‑corruption, or liberation projects and then normalize purges, camps, or show trials as “necessary sacrifices.”

4. **Doomsday Prophecy / End‑Times Logic**  
   - **Fictional Trait:** A known future cataclysm (war, AI uprising, celestial event) is treated as inevitable and near‑term, justifying extreme pre‑emptive violence.  
   - **Historical Inspiration:** Millenarian and apocalyptic movements that see impending collapse as divinely scripted.  
   - **Real‑World Pattern:** Leaders who frame mass violence, suicide, or terror as the only path through an unavoidable end‑times scenario toward salvation.

5. **Charismatic Prophetic Leader**  
   - **Fictional Trait:** A single “Chosen,” “Commander,” or “Prophet” is believed to uniquely interpret fate, alien signals, or superintelligent guidance.  
   - **Historical Inspiration:** Personality cults around political or religious leaders elevated as semi‑divine.  
   - **Real‑World Pattern:** Movements whose internal logic collapses without the leader’s charisma and whose succession crises trigger splinter wars.

6. **Sacred Violence and Purification**  
   - **Fictional Trait:** Killing enemies, mutants, heretics, or “unclean” populations is framed as ritual purification needed to restore the world after war.  
   - **Historical Inspiration:** Ethnic cleansing, inquisitions, and ideological “cleansing” campaigns.  
   - **Real‑World Pattern:** Use of quasi‑religious language to transform mass violence into a sacred duty that overrides moral inhibitions.

7. **Resource‑Scarcity Militarization**  
   - **Fictional Trait:** Water, arable land, energy, or oxygen become military objectives; warlords and militias form around wells, reactors, or seed vaults.  
   - **Historical Inspiration:** Conflicts driven or intensified by competition for food, oil, minerals, or chokepoints.  
   - **Real‑World Pattern:** Actors who fuse logistics and ideology, using control of supply chains as leverage to recruit, punish, or starve populations into submission.

8. **External Scapegoat / Eternal Enemy**  
   - **Fictional Trait:** Collapse is blamed on an out‑group (mutation lineage, off‑world faction, AI caste, ethnic group), making permanent war a moral necessity.  
   - **Historical Inspiration:** Propaganda that casts a group as intrinsic saboteurs or parasites.  
   - **Real‑World Pattern:** Narratives that reinterpret every setback as fresh evidence of an eternal enemy’s conspiracy, preventing de‑escalation.

9. **Information Control and Reality Editing**  
   - **Fictional Trait:** Regimes rewrite archives, censor pre‑war media, and alter digital records to control what survivors believe actually happened.  
   - **Historical Inspiration:** Censorship, propaganda ministries, and historical revisionism in authoritarian states.  
   - **Real‑World Pattern:** Systems that monopolize broadcasting, education, and archives, making it impossible for citizens to cross‑check the “official” story.

10. **Survivor Stratification and Caste Systems**  
    - **Fictional Trait:** Survivors are sorted into castes—surface scavengers, bunker nobility, gene‑clean vs. gene‑tainted—often justified by war‑time exposure.  
    - **Historical Inspiration:** Wartime class reordering, caste systems, and occupation‑era collaborator vs. resister hierarchies.  
    - **Real‑World Pattern:** Post‑conflict societies where access to safety, medicine, or foreign backing hardens into new semi‑hereditary class lines.

11. **Ruin Ecology and Technological Relics**  
    - **Fictional Trait:** Vast ruins and leftover kill‑tech (mines, drones, autonomous forts) define where people can live and who can safely move.  
    - **Historical Inspiration:** Landscapes filled with unexploded ordnance, minefields, and contaminated zones.  
    - **Real‑World Pattern:** Generations adapting to invisible infrastructural hazards, creating new taboos, maps, and black markets around forbidden zones.

12. **Fragmented Micro‑Polities and Warlordism**  
    - **Fictional Trait:** Central authority collapses into dozens of city‑states, clans, or ship‑crews, each with its own law, mythos, and war economy.  
    - **Historical Inspiration:** Power vacuums after state failure, civil wars, and foreign withdrawals.  
    - **Real‑World Pattern:** Patchworks of militias, fiefdoms, and “shadow states” taxing trade, people, and aid flows while fighting and negotiating simultaneously.

13. **Weaponization of Orphan Cohorts and Lost Generations**  
    - **Fictional Trait:** Children born or orphaned during the war are raised as shock troops, zealots, or hacker‑castes with no memory of pre‑collapse norms.  
    - **Historical Inspiration:** Child soldiers, politicized youth wings, and “lost generations” shaped by conflict.  
    - **Real‑World Pattern:** Actors who see war‑traumatized youth as malleable tools, training them as enforcers, fanatics, or deniable paramilitaries.

14. **Continuity‑of‑Government Ghost Structures**  
    - **Fictional Trait:** Hidden bunkers, hardened databanks, or AI overseers preserve fragments of the old state and occasionally intervene.  
    - **Historical Inspiration:** Continuity‑of‑government plans, emergency bunkers, secret archives, intelligence “deep state” structures.  
    - **Real‑World Pattern:** Residual bureaucracies, spy networks, or military units that survive regime collapse and shape post‑war orders from the shadows.

15. **Techno‑Feudal Corporate or Cartel Rule**  
    - **Fictional Trait:** Corporations, guilds, or cartels owning key tech (ships, satellites, fabs) become de facto sovereigns after states fall.  
    - **Historical Inspiration:** Company‑towns, chartered companies, and cartel‑dominated regions.  
    - **Real‑World Pattern:** Non‑state actors that control payrolls, logistics, and security in failed states, effectively replacing governments in everyday life.

16. **Ideological Fossilization / Scripted Society**  
    - **Fictional Trait:** A single war‑era doctrine becomes frozen as sacred law—battlefield rules, emergency decrees, or survival codes govern civilian life centuries later.  
    - **Historical Inspiration:** Emergency laws and wartime propaganda that outlive the conflict that spawned them.  
    - **Real‑World Pattern:** Societies that keep “temporary” security measures, emergency courts, or rationing logics long after the original threat, reshaping culture.

17. **Mythic Founding War and Narrative Capture**  
    - **Fictional Trait:** The collapse war is retold as a mythic founding story—heroes, traitors, and miracles are recast to legitimize the current order.  
    - **Historical Inspiration:** National myths about independence wars, revolutions, and resistance movements.  
    - **Real‑World Pattern:** Regimes that selectively canonize fragments of history to justify borders, castes, or eternal enemies, suppressing contradictory memories.

18. **Sanctified Technology vs. Forbidden Tech**  
    - **Fictional Trait:** Some technologies (bio‑mod, AI, nuclear) are demonized as the cause of collapse, while others (wind, analog, “pure” tools) are sanctified.  
    - **Historical Inspiration:** Post‑disaster technophobia and technophilia, Luddite movements, and strict dual‑use controls.  
    - **Real‑World Pattern:** Cultural lines drawn between “corrupting” and “redeeming” tools, shaping education, research, and trade.

19. **Post‑Collapse Trade Networks and Pirate Economies**  
    - **Fictional Trait:** Smugglers, raiders, and free‑traders stitch together islands of civilization, often with their own law code and honor system.  
    - **Historical Inspiration:** Black markets, blockade runners, and pirate havens in wartime and sanctions regimes.  
    - **Real‑World Pattern:** Durable informal economies that keep people alive while undermining official authority and providing alternate loyalties.

20. **Prevention Blind Spots and Inevitable‑War Narratives**  
    - **Fictional Trait:** Characters insist that war and collapse were “inevitable,” erasing earlier decision points where different choices were possible.  
    - **Historical Inspiration:** Post‑hoc narratives that reframe avoidable conflicts as destiny or necessity.  
    - **Real‑World Pattern:** Societies that normalize fatalism about war, making it harder to identify and act on early warning signs in future crises.

***

## How to use these invariants in Literature%Hero

Each invariant can be treated as a constraint and analysis lens when you process a movie, game, novel, or artwork about war‑driven collapse. For a given work, you can score which invariants are present, how they interact, and where the narrative departs from plausible historical patterns. This supports the project’s goals: separating fact from fiction, identifying realistic failure modes, and drafting counter‑measures or “escape routes” (institutional designs, cultural interventions, technical safeguards) that could reduce the odds of similar collapses in reality.
